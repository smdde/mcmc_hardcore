# DM-2026-019: A Research Evolution History

## Phase 1: Problem Germination (The Entropy Question)

### The Starting Point

The initial question was extremely simple:

> On a random $d$-regular graph, what is the growth law of the entropy of the independent set size $|I|$?

Notation:

$$H_{\mathrm{ind}} = H(|I|)$$

We wanted to understand:

- Is it $O(1)$?
- Is it $\log n$?
- Or is it $n$?

At the time, there was no theoretical framework.

---

## Phase 2: Intuitive Conjecture

Observation:

Many statistical models satisfy the CLT:

$$X \approx N(\mu, \sigma^2)$$

And the entropy of a Gaussian variable:

$$H(X) \approx \frac{1}{2}\log(2\pi e \sigma^2)$$

This led to the initial conjecture:

If

$$\mathrm{Var}(|I|) \sim \chi_d n$$

then:

$$H_{\mathrm{ind}} = \frac{1}{2}\log_2 n + C_d + o(1)$$

where

$$C_d = \frac{1}{2}\log_2(2\pi e \chi_d)$$

---

### Initial Positioning

We believed:

This is a brand-new entropy formula.

---

## Phase 3: Introducing the Cavity Method

To compute $\chi_d$.

Using the Bethe/cavity equations.

Obtained the occupancy fraction:

$$\rho_d$$

Further using the simplest approximation:

$$\chi_d \approx \rho_d(1-\rho_d)$$

Obtained:

### First-Version Formula

$$C_d = \frac{1}{2}\log_2(2\pi e \rho(1-\rho))$$

---

### At the Time, We Believed

The entire project was complete.

---

## Phase 4: Large-$d$ Asymptotics

Continued analysis:

Obtained a Lambert $W$ expansion:

$$C_d = -\frac{1}{2}\log_2 d + 1.047 - \frac{0.721}{W(d)} + \cdots$$

Even computed up to:

$$d = 10^6$$

Error:

$$2 \times 10^{-5}$$

---

### Positioning at the Time

We believed:

This is the project's greatest achievement.

## Phase 9: The $\chi < 0$ Crisis

### Later Discovered

GPT pointed out:

True susceptibility cannot be negative.

$\chi > 0$ must necessarily hold.

Numerical differentiation entered the wrong branch.
